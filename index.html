Adding books

  • In the app: tap the + button at the top of your haven, or open the side menu and choose Import Books. Pick From This Device to browse the Files picker: iCloud Drive, on-device storage, and anything else the Files app can see.
  • From another app: share any book file to BookHaven. AirDrop, Mail attachments, Safari downloads, and the Files app all work this way.
  • From your cloud: connect Dropbox, Google Drive, or OneDrive once under Settings › Cloud Accounts. After that, the import menu offers to browse that service directly.
  • Folders and zips: both import in one go. A folder of MP3 or M4A chapter files becomes a single audiobook, disc layouts (CD 1, CD 2) included.
  • Never empty: seven public-domain classics come bundled, and Discover points to trusted sources of free, DRM-free books and audiobooks.

Supported formats

Ebooks EPUB · KEPUB · PDF · MOBI · AZW3 · FB2 · TXT · RTF
Audiobooks M4B · M4A · MP3, as single files or as folders of MP3/M4A chapters

DRM-protected files don't open. BookHaven reads DRM-free files only.

Kindle or Audible books?

Amazon's DRM means BookHaven can't read Kindle or Audible files directly. DRM-free copies of books you own (EPUB for ebooks, MP3 or M4B for audio) import fine through any of the methods above.

Reading

  • Your place is saved automatically, in every format. The bookmark button in the top bar marks pages; tapping a saved bookmark jumps back and briefly highlights the passage.
  • The Aa panel sets the reading theme (Light, Sepia, and a warm Dark), one of thirteen fonts, text size, spacing, and alignment. Prefer the book's original look? Reset to the publisher's own styling from the same panel.
  • Press and hold any word to select it — then Look Up opens the iOS dictionary (add English, Türkçe, and more once via Manage Dictionaries at the bottom of that panel), Translate handles cross-language reading, and Copy does what it says.
  • PDFs add pinch-zoom, in-document search, and a true dark mode.
  • The first book you open shows a short tour of the reader controls. Replay it, and every other tip, from Settings › Show All Tips Again.

Audiobooks

  • Single files play as they are; a folder of MP3 or M4A chapters imports as one audiobook with its chapters in order.
  • The player has chapter navigation, bookmarks, a sleep timer, and per-book playback speed.
  • Playback continues on the lock screen with full controls, and headphone buttons work.
  • Spatial Audio titles play natively and route to supported headphones and speakers.

The cloud badge on a cover

  • A cloud badge with a download arrow means the book's record is on this device but its file isn't. That's normal after iCloud sync brings your library to a new device. Tap the book and BookHaven fetches the file from the cloud service it came from.
  • A cloud badge with an exclamation mark means the original file seems to have moved or been deleted in your cloud account. Tap the book to reconnect or pick the file again.

iCloud sync (Beta)

  • Off by default in v1.0. Turn it on under Settings › iCloud Sync (Beta); the change takes effect the next time BookHaven launches. Use the same Apple ID on every device.
  • What syncs: your library, bookmarks, reading progress, shelves, and reading preferences.
  • Ebook files are a separate, optional toggle (Sync Ebook Files). Very large files, roughly over 40 MB, are skipped and marked on the book; a cloud drive is the better home for those.
  • Audiobook files never sync through iCloud. Import them from Dropbox, Google Drive, or OneDrive to carry them across devices.

Ads, membership, havens & tips

BookHaven is free, and reading is always free. The free tier shows ads: small banners on the Discover and Stats screens, and an occasional full-screen ad at natural pauses, like opening a book. Ads never interrupt reading, listening, or your haven. The banner also offers a short optional video that earns ad-free time.

Two purchases exist, and they're independent. Buying one does not buy the other:

  • Ad-Free Membership: a monthly or annual subscription that removes ads. It doesn't unlock havens.
  • Premium havens: one-time purchases (The Tower, The Nook, Zen Garden, The Witch's Attic) that unlock that haven forever. They don't remove ads.

The Study is free forever. New users get a 7-day welcome gift of every haven, ad-free. Tips in the Shop (Buy Me a Book, a Shelf, a Library) are pure thank-yous: they unlock nothing, by design.

Bought something on another device, or reinstalled? Tap Restore Purchases in the Shop. The membership can be managed or cancelled anytime through your Apple ID's Subscriptions.

Refunds

App Store payments belong to Apple, so refunds are handled at reportaproblem.apple.com. If a purchase didn't unlock what you expected, write to bookhavensupport@gmail.com first: most "didn't unlock" confusion is about which purchase does what (the membership removes ads; a haven purchase unlocks that haven).

Privacy

  • No account, no sign-in, and no analytics of your reading. BookHaven has no servers; your library, progress, and stats live on your device.
  • iCloud sync, when you turn it on, runs through your private iCloud. Only your Apple ID can reach it.
  • Free-tier ads come from Google AdMob. Your books and reading habits stay out of it, and your answer to the iOS tracking prompt is respected.

Full policy: hcyalcin.github.io/bookhaven-privacy
